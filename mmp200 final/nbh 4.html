Welcome to Elmhurst

Elmhurst, Queens Elmhurst is a working/middle class neighborhood located in the borough of Queens, New York City. It is bounded by Roosevelt Avenue, the Long Island Expressway, Junction Boulevard on the east and Queens Boulevard, Grand Avenue and Broadway run through it. Elmhurst has evolved to a multi-ethnic area with second- and third-generation Americans and by immigrants from Ecuador,Colombia, Korea, Cuba, the Philippines, China, India and Japan. Elmhurst's has a rapidly growing Chinatown. It is the second in Queens as the other Chinatown is located in Flushing. Elmhurst’s Chinatown in Queens has expanded to 45th Avenue and Whitney Avenue as it started of as a small area with chinese shops on broadway. It is the second largest Chinese enclave in Queens, behind Flushing.There are several other Southeast Asian businesses and shops in the area, including Malaysian Chinese, Singaporean Chinese, Indonesian, Thai, and Vietnamese. Past broadway there is one movie theater and small restaurants serving Indian, Greek, Korean, Thai, Chinese, Argentine, Colombian, Italian and Ecuadorian food. Elmhurst also has two shopping malls, the Queens Center and the Queens Place Mall, Macy’s being in both. There are five parks in Elmhurst, and Flushing Meadow Park. It is the site of the annual Colombian, Ecuadorian and Korean festivals. The park has a museum, tennis center, playgrounds, soccer and baseball fields, zoo and Citi Field Stadium.
